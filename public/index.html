How EdenCode Anchor works

Real weather observations from SK farm stations are turned into tamper-evident cryptographic proofs. The raw data stays private; only a 32-byte fingerprint becomes a public, verifiable artifact. Below is a live worked example you can recompute in your own browser — no trust in EdenCode required.

The provenance pipeline

From a physical sensor reading to an artifact anyone can independently verify.

Tempest Farm Station
A real WeatherFlow Tempest sensor on a Saskatchewan farm emits an observation.
Raw Observation
Temperature, wind, rain, pressure & more captured as a JSON record.
Canonicalize
Station, time & data are serialized into one exact, deterministic string.
SHA-256 → Leaf Key
A one-way hash collapses the record into a unique 32-byte fingerprint.
Merkle Batch
Many leaf hashes are combined pairwise into a single Merkle tree.
Anchored Root
The tree's single root hash is stored as the tamper-evident anchor.
Anyone Can Verify
Recompute leaf + proof to the same root — proof without trusting us.

Why it matters

A 32-byte hash, anchored once, turns ephemeral farm telemetry into durable, defensible evidence.

Tamper-evidence

Change a single digit of temperature, wind or rainfall and the SHA-256 fingerprint changes completely — and no longer reconstructs the anchored Merkle root. Any edit, deletion or backdating is mathematically detectable.

Timestamp & provenance

Each record is bound to its station and observation time before hashing. The anchored root proves that this exact reading existed, unaltered, at that moment — a cryptographic chain of custody from sensor to claim.

Data sovereignty

Only opaque 32-byte hashes are ever published. The raw observation — and any commercial insight in it — stays private to the farm. You prove integrity without ever exposing the underlying agronomic data.

Agricultural value

  • Claim defense: hard evidence for crop-insurance & AgriStability frost, drought or hail events.
  • Carbon baselines: auditable weather inputs underpinning carbon-credit & sustainability accounting.
  • Traceability: tamper-proof growing conditions for organic & provenance certification.
  • Smart contracts: trustworthy data feed for parametric insurance & supply-contract triggers.
